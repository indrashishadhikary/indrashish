Aditya Birla Group's ₹4,730 Cr strategy to revive Vodafone Idea

Country’s third largest telecom operator by market share see a turnaround moment as Aditya Birla Group looks to invest in the debt-laden telecom company for about 4730 cr
Suryaja Investments, a Singapore-based entity of ABG has received approval for a preferential allotment of convertible warrants. This will mark fresh capital infusion and Suryaja Investments will hold upto 3.82% stake in Vi, marking a meaningful vote of confidence from the promoter group.
Average revenue per user being a key metric, improved to inr 190 in the quarter from inr 186 in Q3. However, Vi continues to lose subscribers. Overall, the user base declined from 198.2 million a year earlier to 192.8 million.
Vi spent inr 18,000 cr on capex last fiscal to enhance network quality and expand 4G coverage. Separately, the government reduced Vi’s AGR dues by 27% to 64,046 cr, which improves debt manageability in the near term.
Vi expands 5G services across more than 80 cities, a necessary move to stay competitive, though execution at scale remains the key watch item.
